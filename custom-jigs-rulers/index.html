Custom Jigs & Rulers

I almost can’t fathom how other people work with leather straps and don’t have custom jigs like these.

I think what most people do is to very carefully measure one piece of leather, punch holes in that and use it like a jig, but leather is not as stiff or precise as these.

I draw on the leather using a silver leather marking pen and then punch with a rotary punch tool.

The code to generate these is on github. The PDFs it makes, which are suitable for laser cutting are also hosted there for rivets and belt loops. Note that these can sometimes display incorrectly in Illustrator (try turning off GPU acceleration) and in Preview.

To give you a sense of why this might be useful (and please bear with me, these are an old design where the markings are not as good). You often want to put holes exactly vertically centered in a strap, and for wider pieces, you want to put two holes in vertical alignment.

Now I know that first hole is perfectly centered, ~1/4″ away from the edge, and the next two holes are perfectly in a line, ~1.25″ inches from the edge.
